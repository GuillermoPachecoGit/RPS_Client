Morphometric studies have long been using Cartesian coordinates of anatomical points or landmarks  to extract shape information. Shape differences between individuals are usually analyzed by performing a  Procrustes superposition   of the corresponding landmark configurations, and which superposition criterion should be used is a central issue.

Typically, the least squares Procrustes superposition is chosen; it minimizes the sum of squared distances across landmarks , but it is widely known that the result can be misleading  whenever shape differences are located in up to 50% of the landmarks. A  resistant Procrustes superposition, based on a  repeated medians technique (Torcida et al. 2014, Siegel & Benson 1982) is probably the most elegant and efficient alternative.

RPS (Resistant Procrustes Software)  is an application that  implements a compact a nd integrated framework for landmark-based resistant shape analysis of 2D and 3D datasets. Fully based on (Torcida et al. 2014 ), its main functionalities are:
  • a generalized resistant Procrustes superposition ;
  • a coherent resistant distance, to quantify the resulting shape differences, and
  • a resistant Multidimensional Scaling, to obtain a corresponding ordination. 
Least squares counterparts have also been implemented in RPS. In this way, both resistant and least squares approaches can be applied to the same data in order to compare their results.
